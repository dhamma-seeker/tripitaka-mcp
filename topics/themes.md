# Major Themes in the Pāli Canon

Locus classicus + parallel suttas for the foundational teachings most readers come asking about. Each entry names the canonical sutta where the doctrine receives its definitive treatment, plus two or three places where it recurs across the canon. Every sutta_id below has been verified against this server's loaded data via `get_sutta` — you can fetch each one in full through the MCP tools.

> **Scope:** This page covers ten classical themes that recur most often in real research sessions. It is **not exhaustive** — Theravāda doctrine is dense and many subtopics (e.g. Abhidhamma matikā, Vinaya principles, Jātaka morality) deserve their own pages. Suggestions for additions: open an issue on the [repo](https://github.com/dhamma-seeker/tripitaka-mcp/issues).

## Foundational doctrines

### Four Noble Truths (Cattāri Ariyasaccāni)

The first sermon, delivered to the five ascetics at Isipatana (Sarnath) immediately after the Awakening. Lays out *dukkha*, its cause (*samudaya*), its cessation (*nirodha*), and the path leading to cessation (*magga*).

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 56.11 Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn56.11/pli/ms) — 60 segments, "Setting the wheel of Dhamma in motion"
- **Parallel / extended**: [SN 56.31 Sīsapāvana](https://suttacentral.net/sn56.31/pli/ms) (only a handful of leaves), [DN 22 §18-21](https://suttacentral.net/dn22/pli/ms) (the truths embedded in the satipaṭṭhāna treatment), [MN 141 Saccavibhaṅga](https://suttacentral.net/mn141/pli/ms) (Sāriputta's analytical exposition)

### Three Marks of Existence (Tilakkhaṇa)

All conditioned phenomena are impermanent (*anicca*), unsatisfactory (*dukkha*), and not-self (*anattā*). The standard formula:

> *"Sabbe saṅkhārā aniccā" / "Sabbe saṅkhārā dukkhā" / "Sabbe dhammā anattā."*

- **Locus classicus**: [AN 3.136 Uppādā Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/an3.136/pli/ms) — 19 segments, the formula in its purest declarative form ("whether Tathāgatas arise or not, this property stands…")
- **Parallel / extended**: [SN 22.59 Anattalakkhaṇa Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn22.59/pli/ms) (the second sermon — applies the marks to each of the five aggregates), [Dhp 277-279](https://suttacentral.net/dhp273-289/pli/ms) (verse summaries)

### Dependent Origination (Paṭiccasamuppāda)

The 12-link chain explaining how *dukkha* arises through ignorance, formations, consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense bases, contact, feeling, craving, clinging, becoming, birth, ageing-and-death.

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 12.1 Paṭiccasamuppāda Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn12.1/pli/ms) — 42 segments, the bare 12-link formula in both forward and reverse order
- **Parallel / extended**: [SN 12.2 Vibhaṅga](https://suttacentral.net/sn12.2/pli/ms) (analytical breakdown of each link), [DN 15 Mahānidāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn15/pli/ms) (the Buddha's longest treatment, with Ānanda), [MN 38 Mahātaṇhāsaṅkhaya](https://suttacentral.net/mn38/pli/ms) (correcting Sāti's misunderstanding of consciousness)

## The Self & the aggregates

### Five Aggregates (Pañcakkhandhā)

Form, feeling, perception, formations, consciousness — the five clung-to aggregates that make up what is conventionally called "a person." Used as the analytical frame for *anattā*.

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 22.59 Anattalakkhaṇa Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn22.59/pli/ms) — 59 segments, the second sermon, where each aggregate is examined and shown to be not-self
- **Parallel / extended**: [SN 22 Khandhasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn22) (the entire Saṁyutta — 159 suttas dedicated to the aggregates), [MN 109 Mahāpuṇṇama](https://suttacentral.net/mn109/pli/ms) (Q&A on the aggregates with a senior monk)

## The Path

### Noble Eightfold Path (Ariya Aṭṭhaṅgika Magga)

The fourth Noble Truth made operational: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration. The "middle way" between sensual indulgence and self-mortification.

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 45.8 Vibhaṅga Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn45.8/pli/ms) — 44 segments, each factor analytically defined ("And what, monks, is right view? …")
- **Parallel / extended**: [DN 22 §21](https://suttacentral.net/dn22/pli/ms) (path as the fourth truth, embedded in the satipaṭṭhāna structure), [SN 45.1-180 Maggasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn45) (the dedicated Saṁyutta on the path), [MN 117 Mahācattārīsaka](https://suttacentral.net/mn117/pli/ms) (the path as a system, mundane and supramundane)

### Four Foundations of Mindfulness (Cattāro Satipaṭṭhānā)

Mindfulness of body, feelings, mind, and dhammas — the practical training for the seventh path-factor.

- **Locus classicus**: [DN 22 Mahāsatipaṭṭhāna Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/dn22/pli/ms) — 454 segments, the most extended treatment, including all twenty-one body contemplations and the Four Noble Truths as the final dhamma section
- **Parallel / extended**: [MN 10 Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/mn10/pli/ms) (the same teaching minus the truths section), [SN 47 Satipaṭṭhānasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn47) (104 short suttas on satipaṭṭhāna)

### Mindfulness of Breathing (Ānāpānassati)

Sixteen contemplations grouped into four tetrads — the Buddha's most-recommended subject for serenity-and-insight practice. Note: the literal word `ānāpāna` mostly appears in section markers; the body of teaching uses `assasati` / `passasati` / `dīghaṁ` / `rassaṁ`. A keyword-only search for "ānāpāna" will miss most of the actual content (use `search_hybrid` instead).

- **Locus classicus**: [MN 118 Ānāpānassati Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/mn118/pli/ms) — 154 segments, the sixteen contemplations + how they fulfil the four satipaṭṭhānas + the seven awakening factors
- **Parallel / extended**: [DN 22 §3](https://suttacentral.net/dn22/pli/ms) (the breathing-tetrad inside Kāyānupassanā), [SN 54 Ānāpānasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn54) (20 dedicated suttas, including SN 54.13's parallel of MN 118)

## Mental cultivation

### Loving-kindness (Mettā)

Radiating goodwill to all beings, in all directions, without exception or distinction. The first of the four *brahmavihāra* (divine abidings).

- **Locus classicus**: [Snp 1.8 Karaṇīyamettasutta](https://suttacentral.net/snp1.8/pli/ms) — 43 segments, the verses chanted nightly in Theravāda monasteries ("*as a mother would risk her life to protect her child…*")
- **Parallel / extended**: [AN 11.16 Mettānisaṁsa](https://suttacentral.net/an11.16/pli/ms) (the eleven benefits of mettā cultivation), [MN 21 Kakacūpama](https://suttacentral.net/mn21/pli/ms) (the saw simile — even being sawn limb from limb is no excuse to abandon mettā), [Dhp 5](https://suttacentral.net/dhp1-20/pli/ms) ("*hatred is never appeased by hatred…*")

### Five Hindrances (Pañcanīvaraṇāni)

Sense desire, ill-will, sloth-and-torpor, restlessness-and-worry, doubt — the five mental factors that obstruct concentration and insight.

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 46.51 Āhāra Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn46.51/pli/ms) — 103 segments, what feeds the hindrances vs what feeds the awakening factors (a long comparative analysis)
- **Parallel / extended**: [DN 2 §68-74](https://suttacentral.net/dn2/pli/ms) (the standard hindrance-abandonment sequence in the gradual training), [AN 5.51-55 Nīvaraṇavagga](https://suttacentral.net/an5.51) (a vagga of five suttas dedicated to the hindrances), [MN 39 Mahā-Assapura](https://suttacentral.net/mn39/pli/ms) (the hindrances as five debts/illnesses)

### Seven Factors of Awakening (Satta Bojjhaṅgā)

Mindfulness, investigation, energy, joy, tranquility, concentration, equanimity — the qualities cultivated to the highest degree on the path.

- **Locus classicus**: [SN 46 Bojjhaṅgasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn46) — 184 dedicated suttas; [SN 46.51](https://suttacentral.net/sn46.51/pli/ms) is the comparative-analysis classic
- **Parallel / extended**: [MN 118](https://suttacentral.net/mn118/pli/ms) (how breathing meditation fulfils all seven factors), [DN 22 §17](https://suttacentral.net/dn22/pli/ms) (the awakening factors as a *dhamma-anupassanā* contemplation)

## How to verify any claim on this page

Every sutta_id linked above can be fetched in full through this server's tools. From any MCP-capable client:

```
get_sutta(sutta_id="sn56.11", language="all")
```

The response carries the same `cross_reference.suttacentral.pali_url` linked here, plus segment-by-segment Pāli + English text. If you ever doubt a claim — quote the actual segment.
