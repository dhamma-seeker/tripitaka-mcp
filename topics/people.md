# People in the Pāli Canon

Major figures whose names recur across the suttas: chief disciples, the Buddha's family, lay supporters, kings, brahmins, and notable opponents. Each entry names the figure's role and points to one or two suttas where they appear most prominently — verified against this server's loaded data via `get_sutta`.

> **Scope:** This is a curated list of the ~30 figures who recur most often, not an exhaustive prosopography. The canon mentions hundreds of named individuals once or twice in passing (especially in the Apadāna and Jātaka). For comprehensive treatment, cross-reference with [Malalasekera's *Dictionary of Pāli Proper Names*](https://www.palikanon.com/english/pali_names/dic_idx.html) (DPPN). Suggestions for additions: open an issue on the [repo](https://github.com/dhamma-seeker/tripitaka-mcp/issues).

## The Buddha

Gotama (Sanskrit: Gautama) of the Sakyan clan, also known as Sākyamuni, Bhagavā, Tathāgata. Born at [Lumbinī](places.md#the-four-holy-sites-saṁvegasthāna), awakened at Bodh Gayā, taught for 45 years across the [Mahājanapada](places.md#by-mahājanapada-16-ancient-kingdoms), passed into Parinibbāna at Kusinārā.

- **Biographical foundation**: [DN 14 Mahāpadāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn14/pli/ms) (the lineage of seven Buddhas, with Gotama's birth narrative), [MN 26 Ariyapariyesanā](https://suttacentral.net/mn26/pli/ms) (his own first-person account of the renunciation and awakening), [DN 16 Mahāparinibbāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms) (the final months and Parinibbāna)

## Chief monastic disciples

### Sāriputta (Sanskrit: Śāriputra)

Foremost in wisdom (*paññā*). Right-hand disciple. Many of the canon's analytical/abhidhammic discourses are his.

- [MN 28 Mahāhatthipadopama](https://suttacentral.net/mn28/pli/ms) — his great elephant-footprint exposition of the four truths via the four elements
- [MN 141 Saccavibhaṅga](https://suttacentral.net/mn141/pli/ms) — his analytical treatment of the Four Noble Truths (the Buddha endorses it as "as if I myself had spoken it")
- His passing: [SN 47.13 Cunda](https://suttacentral.net/sn47.13/pli/ms) (Cundā Samaṇuddesa brings news of Sāriputta's death to the Buddha)

### Mahāmoggallāna (Sanskrit: Mahā-Maudgalyāyana)

Foremost in psychic powers (*iddhi*). Sāriputta's lifelong companion since both were students of Sañjaya.

- [SN 21.1 Kolita](https://suttacentral.net/sn21.1/pli/ms) — his account of attaining the formless attainments and liberation
- [MN 50 Māratajjanīya](https://suttacentral.net/mn50/pli/ms) — Moggallāna confronts Māra, recalling his previous life as Māra in another aeon

### Mahākassapa

Foremost in ascetic practices (*dhutaṅga*). Convened the First Council after the Parinibbāna.

- [SN 16 Kassapasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn16) — 13 dedicated suttas; [SN 16.5 Jiṇṇa](https://suttacentral.net/sn16.5/pli/ms) is the famous "old age" exchange where the Buddha asks why he keeps to ascetic practices
- [SN 16.11 Cīvara](https://suttacentral.net/sn16.11/pli/ms) — his ordination story and exchange of robes with the Buddha

### Ānanda

The Buddha's personal attendant for 25 years. Foremost in five categories including learning (*bahussuta*) and memory.

- [DN 16 Mahāparinibbāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms) — his most extensive presence; the Buddha's final months told largely through his eyes
- [MN 88 Bāhitika](https://suttacentral.net/mn88/pli/ms) — his Q&A with King Pasenadi
- [SN 22.83 Ānanda](https://suttacentral.net/sn22.83/pli/ms) — his realisation talk under Puṇṇa Mantāniputta

### Anuruddha

Foremost in the divine eye (*dibbacakkhu*). The Buddha's cousin.

- [MN 127 Anuruddha](https://suttacentral.net/mn127/pli/ms) — exposition on the immeasurable mind vs the exalted mind
- [MN 128 Upakkilesa](https://suttacentral.net/mn128/pli/ms) — the Buddha's teaching to him on the eleven defilements obstructing concentration

### Upāli (the monk)

Foremost in Vinaya. A barber by birth — his ordination established that Vinaya knowledge transcends caste.

- [Vinaya Parivāra ch. 1.1](https://suttacentral.net/pli-tv-pvr1.1/pli/ms) (`pli-tv-pvr1.1`) — opening of the Vinaya analytical compendium often attributed to Upāli's tradition; the full Parivāra is loaded under the `pli-tv-pvr*` ID family
- [AN 7.83 Satthusāsana](https://suttacentral.net/an7.83/pli/ms) — the seven principles he received as criteria for what counts as the Buddha's teaching

## Female disciples (etadagga among bhikkhunīs)

### Mahāpajāpatī Gotamī

The Buddha's foster mother. Foremost in seniority among bhikkhunīs.

- [AN 8.51 Gotamī](https://suttacentral.net/an8.51/pli/ms) — her ordination story and the eight rules (*aṭṭha garudhammā*)
- [Thig 6.6 Mahāpajāpatī Gotamī](https://suttacentral.net/thig6.6/pli/ms) — her own verse

### Khemā

Foremost in wisdom among bhikkhunīs. Former chief queen of King Bimbisāra.

- [SN 44.1 Khemā](https://suttacentral.net/sn44.1/pli/ms) — her famous exchange with King Pasenadi about whether the Tathāgata exists after death (she compares the Tathāgata's depth to the ocean's)

### Uppalavaṇṇā

Foremost in psychic powers among bhikkhunīs.

- [Thig 5.5 Uppalavaṇṇā](https://suttacentral.net/thig5.5/pli/ms) — her verse, including her resistance to a would-be assailant

### Dhammadinnā

Foremost in expounding Dhamma among bhikkhunīs. The Buddha endorsed her teaching as if it were his own.

- [MN 44 Cūḷavedalla](https://suttacentral.net/mn44/pli/ms) — her catechism with her former husband Visākha (a different Visākha from the lay supporter)

## Lay supporters

### Anāthapiṇḍika

The Buddha's chief male lay supporter. Donated Jetavana to the Sangha — the most-cited setting in the canon.

- [SN 55.26 Anāthapiṇḍika 1](https://suttacentral.net/sn55.26/pli/ms) — his sickness and visit from Sāriputta
- [MN 143 Anāthapiṇḍikovāda](https://suttacentral.net/mn143/pli/ms) — Sāriputta's teaching on his deathbed; Anāthapiṇḍika is reborn in Tusita

### Visākhā

The Buddha's chief female lay supporter. Donated Pubbārāma (Migāramātupāsāda) to the Sangha.

- [AN 8.43 Visākhā](https://suttacentral.net/an8.43/pli/ms) — the eight-factor uposatha observance, taught to her
- [Ud 8.8 Visākhā](https://suttacentral.net/ud8.8/pli/ms) — the consolation when her granddaughter dies

### Citta the householder

Foremost in expounding Dhamma among lay disciples.

- [SN 41 Cittasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn41) — 10 dedicated suttas, including [SN 41.1 Saṁyojana](https://suttacentral.net/sn41.1/pli/ms) where he correctly teaches monks the difference between the fetters and what is fettered

### Jīvaka Komārabhacca

The Buddha's physician. Donated his Mango Grove (Jīvakambavana) for the Sangha at Rājagaha.

- [MN 55 Jīvaka](https://suttacentral.net/mn55/pli/ms) — his question about whether monks may eat meat (the threefold rule)

## Kings

### Bimbisāra of Magadha

Donor of Veḷuvana (the first Buddhist monastery). Father of Ajātasattu.

- [Snp 3.1 Pabbajjā](https://suttacentral.net/snp3.1/pli/ms) — his first meeting with the Bodhisatta on Mount Paṇḍava (pre-awakening)

### Pasenadi of Kosala

Frequent visitor of the Buddha — many of his exchanges are recorded.

- [SN 3 Kosalasaṁyutta](https://suttacentral.net/sn3) — 25 dedicated suttas; [SN 3.1 Daharaṁ](https://suttacentral.net/sn3.1/pli/ms) is the first meeting (the four "young things not to be despised" — a young snake, a young fire, a young noble, a young monk)
- [MN 87 Piyajātika](https://suttacentral.net/mn87/pli/ms) — the teaching that "from the loved springs grief"

### Ajātasattu of Magadha

Bimbisāra's son who killed his father to take the throne. Later became a lay supporter; survived the schism crisis.

- [DN 2 Sāmaññaphala](https://suttacentral.net/dn2/pli/ms) — his night visit to the Buddha, the question on the visible fruits of recluseship, and his confession of patricide

## The Buddha's family

- **Suddhodana** — his father, king of the Sakyans
- **Mahāmāyā** — his mother (passed away seven days after his birth, reborn in Tāvatiṁsa). The Buddha later visits Tāvatiṁsa to teach her the Abhidhamma (commentarial tradition; the canon mentions the visit briefly)
- **Mahāpajāpatī Gotamī** — her sister, his foster mother (see [above](#mahāpajāpatī-gotamī))
- **Yasodharā** — his wife before renunciation; ordained later as Bhaddakaccānā
- **Rāhula** — his son
  - [MN 61 Ambalaṭṭhikarāhulovāda](https://suttacentral.net/mn61/pli/ms) — the rinsing-water and elephant similes on truthfulness
  - [MN 62 Mahārāhulovāda](https://suttacentral.net/mn62/pli/ms) — the elements meditation
  - [MN 147 Cūḷarāhulovāda](https://suttacentral.net/mn147/pli/ms) — his final teaching to Rāhula leading to arahantship
- **Nanda** — his half-brother (Suddhodana + Mahāpajāpatī's son)

## Notable converts

### Aṅgulimāla

Bandit who wore a garland (*mālā*) of fingers (*aṅguli*) from his victims. Converted by the Buddha and became an arahant.

- [MN 86 Aṅgulimāla](https://suttacentral.net/mn86/pli/ms) — his story; the famous "I have stopped" exchange ("I have stopped harming; you have not stopped")

### Bāhiya Dārucīriya

The "bark-cloth wearer" who attained arahantship in the time it took the Buddha to deliver three sentences ("In the seen, only the seen…"). Killed by a cow shortly after.

- [Ud 1.10 Bāhiya](https://suttacentral.net/ud1.10/pli/ms) — his story

### Upāli the householder

A wealthy Jain lay-follower sent to debate the Buddha. Converted instead and tried to switch alms-giving to the Buddha (the Buddha refused, telling him to keep supporting the Jains too).

- [MN 56 Upāli](https://suttacentral.net/mn56/pli/ms) — the conversion story

### Sigālaka (Sigāla)

Young householder who worshipped the six directions on his father's instruction without understanding why. Buddha gave him the lay code of ethics.

- [DN 31 Sigālovāda](https://suttacentral.net/dn31/pli/ms) — the "lay vinaya"

## Opponents and contested figures

### Devadatta

The Buddha's cousin who tried to take over the Sangha, attempted murder, and split the community.

- [Iti 89 Devadatta](https://suttacentral.net/iti89/pli/ms) — Devadatta's eight evil qualities
- [Vinaya Cullavagga ch. 7](https://suttacentral.net/pli-tv-kd17/pli/ms) (`pli-tv-kd17`) — the schism narrative

### The six heretical teachers (cha satthāro)

Six contemporary spiritual teachers whose views the Buddha rejected: Pūraṇa Kassapa, Makkhali Gosāla, Ajita Kesakambala, Pakudha Kaccāyana, Sañjaya Belaṭṭhiputta, and the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta (Mahāvīra of the Jains).

- [DN 2 Sāmaññaphala §16-33](https://suttacentral.net/dn2/pli/ms) — King Ajātasattu describes each teacher's views to the Buddha

### Brahmā Sahampati

A Brahmā deva (not human) who appears at three pivotal moments: convincing the newly-awakened Buddha to teach (since some have "little dust in their eyes"), rebuking Mahābrahmā in [DN 11 Kevaṭṭa](https://suttacentral.net/dn11/pli/ms), and announcing the Parinibbāna.

- [SN 6.1 Brahmāyācana](https://suttacentral.net/sn6.1/pli/ms) — his entreaty to teach

## How to verify any claim on this page

Every sutta_id linked above can be fetched in full through this server's tools. From any MCP-capable client:

```text
get_sutta(sutta_id="mn86", language="all")
```

The response carries the same `cross_reference.suttacentral.pali_url` linked here, plus segment-by-segment Pāli + English text. For prose biographies and cross-tradition context, [DPPN](https://www.palikanon.com/english/pali_names/dic_idx.html) is the standard external reference.
