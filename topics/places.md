# Places in the Pāli Canon (Tipiṭaka)

> Geography of the suttas — major loci that recur across the canon. For a comprehensive prosopography of single-mention place names, cross-reference with [Malalasekera's *Dictionary of Pali Proper Names*](https://www.palikanon.com/english/pali_names/dic_idx.html) (DPPN). To compile something custom, use the MCP server tools and the [tipitaka-research](../skills/tipitaka-research.md) skill — that workflow reproduces consistent results.

## Scope and disclaimers

- Sources: Tipitaka MCP server (verified via `search_by_keyword` against the Pāli text) + cross-reference with [SuttaCentral](https://suttacentral.net) and Malalasekera's *Dictionary of Pali Proper Names* (DPPN)
- Modern locations are inferred from archaeology and scholarly consensus — some sites remain disputed (notably Kapilavatthu: Tilaurakot in Nepal vs Piprahwa in India)
- The page covers the major loci that recur in the canon — it is **not exhaustive**. Place names appearing in single Jātaka stories, single Apadāna verses, or short Vinaya passages are not all listed
- Atthakathā (commentarial) traditions about places are not covered — they belong in a separate page

## The Four Holy Sites (Saṁvegasthāna)

Defined in [DN 16 Mahāparinibbāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms) as places that should arouse *saṁvega* (spiritual urgency) for the faithful.

| Site | Pāli | Event | Modern location |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lumbinī | Lumbinī | Birth of the Buddha | Lumbinī, Nepal |
| Bodh Gayā | Uruvelā / Bodhimaṇḍa | Awakening | Bodh Gaya, Bihar, India |
| Sarnath | Isipatana Migadāya | First sermon ([SN 56.11](https://suttacentral.net/sn56.11/pli/ms)) | Sarnath, Uttar Pradesh, India |
| Kushinagar | Kusinārā Upavattana Sālavana | Final Nibbāna ([DN 16](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms)) | Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh, India |

## By Mahājanapada (16 ancient kingdoms)

The Buddha's life and teaching took place across the *mahājanapada* — the 16 large states of Iron-Age North India. The major centres listed below are confirmed in the canon and recur across hundreds of suttas; smaller villages and visit sites are best looked up via the MCP server.

### Magadha (capital: Rājagaha — modern Rajgir, Bihar)

The kingdom where the Buddha attained Awakening. Major locations include **Veḷuvana** (the first Buddhist monastery, gifted by King Bimbisāra), **Gijjhakūṭa** (Vulture Peak), **Jīvaka's Mango Grove**, **Nāḷandā**, **Pāṭaligāma** (later Pāṭaliputta, Aśoka's capital), and **Uruvelā** (the area where the Buddha awakened).

### Kosala (capital: Sāvatthī — modern Saheth-Maheth, UP)

The kingdom where the Buddha spent the most time. **Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍikārāma** is the most-cited setting in the canon (Anāthapiṇḍika's monastery). Other Sāvatthī monasteries: **Pubbārāma** (Migāramātupāsāda — Visākhā's foundation), **Rājakārāma**, **Andhavana**.

### Vajji (federal capital: Vesālī — modern Vaishali, Bihar)

A confederation of clans. **Mahāvana Kūṭāgārasālā** (the Hall with the Peaked Roof in the Great Wood) hosted many discourses; **Ambapālīvana** (the courtesan Ambapālī's mango grove); **Cāpāla cetiya** (where the Buddha relinquished his life-force, [DN 16](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms)).

### Sakya (capital: Kapilavatthu — modern site disputed, Nepal-India border)

The Buddha's clan homeland. **Nigrodhārāma**, **Mahāvana**, **Lumbinī**.

### Malla (Kusinārā Mallas / Pāvā Mallas)

**Kusinārā Sālavana** — site of Parinibbāna. **Pāvā** — where the Buddha received his final meal from Cunda the smith.

### Other kingdoms with significant scenes

- **Kāsi** — Bārāṇasī (Varanasi), site of Isipatana / Sarnath
- **Vaṁsa** — Kosambī (Allahabad area), with **Ghositārāma** monastery
- **Avanti** — capital Ujjenī (Ujjain), notable for Bhikkhu Mahākaccāyana's residence
- **Aṅga** — Campā (near Bhagalpur)
- **Bhagga** — Suṁsumāragira
- **Kuru** — Kammāsadhamma (where DN 22 / MN 10 Mahāsatipaṭṭhāna was taught)
- **Cetiya**, **Sumbha**, **Verañjā**, **Madhurā**, **Sunāparanta**, **Āḷavī**

## Mountains and rivers

**Five mountains around Rājagaha** (*Pañcagiri*): Vebhāra (with the Sattapaṇṇi cave where the First Council was held), Paṇḍava, Vepulla, Gijjhakūṭa, Isigili (see [MN 116](https://suttacentral.net/mn116/pli/ms)).

**Five great rivers** (*mahānadi-pañcaka*) — see [AN 7.66](https://suttacentral.net/an7.66/pli/ms): Gaṅgā (Ganges), Yamunā (Jamuna), Aciravatī (modern Rapti), Sarabhū (Sarayu / Ghaghara), Mahī (Gandak / Mahi).

## Cosmological realms

Not geographical locations but realms of existence (*loka*) that recur in the canon as settings for events (devas visiting, the Buddha visiting Tāvatiṁsa to teach his mother, etc.). Listed by the standard Theravāda 31-realm scheme.

| Group | Realms | Reference |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sense-sphere heavens (6) | Cātumahārājika, Tāvatiṁsa, Yāma, Tusita, Nimmānarati, Paranimmita-vasavatti | [DN 14](https://suttacentral.net/dn14/pli/ms), [DN 18](https://suttacentral.net/dn18/pli/ms), [DN 21](https://suttacentral.net/dn21/pli/ms) |
| Form realms (16) | Brahmā realms including the *Suddhāvāsa* (Pure Abodes: Avihā, Atappā, Sudassā, Sudassī, Akaniṭṭhā) | [DN 1](https://suttacentral.net/dn1/pli/ms), [MN 49](https://suttacentral.net/mn49/pli/ms) |
| Formless realms (4) | Ākāsānañcāyatana, Viññāṇañcāyatana, Ākiñcaññāyatana, Nevasaññānāsaññāyatana | [AN 4.123](https://suttacentral.net/an4.123/pli/ms) |
| Lower realms (4) | Niraya, Tiracchānayoni, Pittivisaya, Asurakāya | [MN 130](https://suttacentral.net/mn130/pli/ms) |

## Compiling a fresh exhaustive list

If you need a comprehensive list right now, use the MCP server with the [`tipitaka-research`](../skills/tipitaka-research.md) skill loaded. The proven workflow:

1. Run `list_structure` to confirm coverage
2. Search-verify a batch of 8-10 well-known place names (`Jetavane`, `Veḷuvane`, `Isipatane`, `Kusinārāyaṁ`, `Kapilavatthusmiṁ`, etc.) to discover what's in the data
3. Iterate by Mahājanapada, asking the AI to compile each kingdom's locations
4. Cross-reference with DPPN for modern locations and disputed identifications

This page will be updated to fold in those compilations as they stabilise.
