# Timeline of the Buddha's Life and Mission

A phase-based timeline anchored on canonical events. The Pāli Canon does not date events linearly — there is no year-by-year chronology in the suttas themselves. What follows uses only events that the canon itself records with clear context, organized into six phases. Where the canon's evidence is silent and the standard chronology relies on Buddhaghosa's Atthakathā or the Sri Lankan chronicles, that is flagged explicitly at the end.

> **Scope:** Canonical events only — every sutta_id below is fetchable through this server. For year-by-year vassa locations, exact regnal dates of contemporary kings, and other chronological details, see the [Atthakathā](https://www.palikanon.com/english/pali_names/dic_idx.html) (commentarial) and Mahāvaṃsa (Sri Lankan chronicle) sources, which are *not* loaded here.

## Phase 1 — Renunciation and striving

The Buddha-to-be (Bodhisatta) leaves the householder life around age 29. His first-person accounts of the renunciation and the six years of striving are in the suttas themselves, not just biographical commentary.

- Birth at [Lumbinī](places.md#the-four-holy-sites-saṁvegasthāna) into the Sakyan clan — the lineage and birth-pattern of past Buddhas in [DN 14 Mahāpadāna](https://suttacentral.net/dn14/pli/ms) (the Buddha narrates his predecessors and references his own birth)
- The going forth — first-person account in [MN 26 Ariyapariyesanā](https://suttacentral.net/mn26/pli/ms) ("Why, being myself subject to birth, do I seek what is also subject to birth?")
- Six years of striving under Āḷāra Kālāma, Uddaka Rāmaputta, then alone:
  - Extreme austerities described in detail in [MN 36 Mahāsaccaka](https://suttacentral.net/mn36/pli/ms)
  - Solitary practice at Uruvelā in [MN 4 Bhayabherava](https://suttacentral.net/mn4/pli/ms) (overcoming fear in remote places)
  - Confrontation with Māra in [Snp 3.2 Padhāna](https://suttacentral.net/snp3.2/pli/ms) — Māra's army and the Bodhisatta's resolve
- Abandoning austerities, recalling jhāna under the rose-apple tree (MN 36, MN 26)

## Phase 2 — Awakening and the first turning of the wheel

The Awakening at Uruvelā (later "Bodh Gayā") and the seven weeks that followed are described in both the suttas and the Vinaya. The first sermon and the formation of the first five-monk Sangha mark the beginning of the teaching mission.

- The three watches of the night and full Awakening — narrated in [MN 36 Mahāsaccaka](https://suttacentral.net/mn36/pli/ms) §38+ and [MN 26 Ariyapariyesanā](https://suttacentral.net/mn26/pli/ms) §18+
- Seven weeks at the Bodhi tree — [Ud 2.1 Mucalinda](https://suttacentral.net/ud2.1/pli/ms) (the nāga king sheltering the Buddha during a storm)
- Hesitation to teach + Brahmā Sahampati's request — [SN 6.1 Brahmāyācana](https://suttacentral.net/sn6.1/pli/ms)
- Journey to Isipatana (modern Sarnath)
- **First sermon — Setting the Wheel of Dhamma in motion**: [SN 56.11 Dhammacakkappavattana](https://suttacentral.net/sn56.11/pli/ms) — the Four Noble Truths to the five ascetics
- **Second sermon — the Characteristic of Not-Self**: [SN 22.59 Anattalakkhaṇa](https://suttacentral.net/sn22.59/pli/ms) — the five become arahants

## Phase 3 — Early formation of the Sangha

The Vinaya's Mahāvagga (the "Great Section") tells most of these early-mission stories in narrative sequence. The opening *khandhakas* are essentially the Buddha's first teaching tour.

- Yasa of Vārāṇasī and his 54 friends — first lay conversions and arahant ordinations ([Mahāvagga ch.1, pli-tv-kd1](https://suttacentral.net/pli-tv-kd1/pli/ms))
- The 60 first arahants sent out: "*Caratha bhikkhave cārikaṁ bahujanahitāya …*" — "Wander forth, monks, for the welfare of the many"
- The Bhaddavaggiya (the "auspicious group") of 30 noble youths
- The three Kassapa brothers + their 1,000 fire-worshipping ascetics, converted by the fire-element miracle (still in `pli-tv-kd1`)
- King Bimbisāra's conversion at Veḷuvana — first donation of a monastery to the Sangha (`pli-tv-kd1`); the pre-awakening encounter with Bimbisāra is in [Snp 3.1 Pabbajjā](https://suttacentral.net/snp3.1/pli/ms)
- **Sāriputta and Moggallāna ordained** — they were students of Sañjaya at Rājagaha when Sāriputta met Assaji and heard the Dhamma summary (`pli-tv-kd1`); cited [SN 21.1 Kolita](https://suttacentral.net/sn21.1/pli/ms) for Moggallāna's later realisation account
- Mahākassapa joins — [SN 16.11 Cīvara](https://suttacentral.net/sn16.11/pli/ms)

## Phase 4 — Mid-career establishment

Suttas in this phase are harder to date relative to each other — the canon mostly tells stories without timestamps. What we can say: the Sangha's institutional foundations (Jetavana, the bhikkhunīsaṅgha) are formed in this period, and many of the most-cited individual conversions happen here.

- Anāthapiṇḍika's gift of Jetavana — [Cullavagga ch.6 Senāsanakkhandhaka, pli-tv-kd16](https://suttacentral.net/pli-tv-kd16/pli/ms) (the donation narrative + the famous "covering-with-gold-pieces" story)
- Suddhodana's death (the Buddha's father)
- Mahāpajāpatī's request for women's ordination — [AN 8.51 Gotamī](https://suttacentral.net/an8.51/pli/ms): the eight rules (*aṭṭha garudhammā*) and the founding of the bhikkhunīsaṅgha
- [Aṅgulimāla's conversion](https://suttacentral.net/mn86/pli/ms) (MN 86) — the bandit who became an arahant
- [Bāhiya Dārucīriya's quick awakening](https://suttacentral.net/ud1.10/pli/ms) (Ud 1.10) — three sentences, full liberation, killed by a cow shortly after
- The visit to Tāvatiṁsa to teach the Abhidhamma to Mahāmāyā — referenced briefly in canon (e.g. [DN 21 Sakkapañha](https://suttacentral.net/dn21/pli/ms) shows the Buddha there); the three-month sojourn is filled out in commentary

## Phase 5 — Late career: the Devadatta crisis

Devadatta's attempt to take over the Sangha and his subsequent schism are dated by the Vinaya itself to a specific period — usually placed in the Buddha's last decade. The schism narrative is one of the longest sustained passages in the Vinaya.

- Devadatta requests to lead the Sangha — Buddha refuses, citing Devadatta's lack of qualities ([Iti 89](https://suttacentral.net/iti89/pli/ms))
- Failed attempts on the Buddha's life (rolling a boulder, sending the elephant Nāḷāgiri)
- The schism — Devadatta's "five ascetic rules" and the splitting of 500 monks ([Cullavagga ch.7 Saṅghabhedakkhandhaka, pli-tv-kd17](https://suttacentral.net/pli-tv-kd17/pli/ms))
- Sāriputta and Moggallāna recover the schismatic monks
- Devadatta's death

A useful chronological anchor: Mahāvīra (the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, founder of the Jains) dies during the Buddha's last few years — [MN 104 Sāmagāma](https://suttacentral.net/mn104/pli/ms) and [DN 29 Pāsādika](https://suttacentral.net/dn29/pli/ms) are both delivered in response to news of his death and the Jain schism that followed. These suttas are explicit timing markers.

## Phase 6 — The final months

[DN 16 Mahāparinibbāna Sutta](https://suttacentral.net/dn16/pli/ms) — at 1,664 segments the longest single narrative in the canon — gives a continuous account of roughly the last twelve months. It is the only canonical text with sustained day-by-day chronology. The sequence:

- Departure from Rājagaha; the Vajjian Council
- The journey north — Pāṭaligāma (later Pāṭaliputta), crossing the Ganges
- Vassa at Beluvagāmaka — the Buddha's grave illness, his decision to live until parinibbāna
- Cāpāla cetiya near Vesālī — relinquishing the life-force (*āyusaṅkhāra*)
- News of [Sāriputta's death](https://suttacentral.net/sn47.13/pli/ms) (SN 47.13) and [the joint reference to both chief disciples gone](https://suttacentral.net/sn47.14/pli/ms) (SN 47.14)
- The journey through Bhoganagara, Pāvā
- The last meal at Cunda the smith's house (*sūkaramaddava*)
- Final illness at Hiraññavatī river
- Arrival at Kusinārā Sālavana
- The exchange with Subhadda — the Buddha's last personal disciple
- Last words: "*vayadhammā saṅkhārā, appamādena sampādetha*" — "All formations are subject to decay; strive on with diligence"
- Parinibbāna at Kusinārā Sālavana, the third watch of the night

## What's commentarial (not in this server)

The standard biographical chronology you'll see in popular treatments draws heavily on later sources:

- **45-year vassa locations** — assigned year-by-year in Buddhaghosa's Atthakathā (5th c. CE). The canon attests *which* monasteries the Buddha used (Veḷuvana, Jetavana, Pubbārāma, Migāramātupāsāda, etc.) but not which year he stayed where.
- **Dates in BCE** — derived from Sri Lankan chronicles (Dīpavaṃsa, Mahāvaṃsa) cross-referenced with Aśokan inscriptions. Two main schools: long chronology (~566-486 BCE) vs short chronology (~448-368 BCE). Modern scholarship leans toward the short chronology.
- **Eight pivotal events / four pilgrimage sites** — DN 16 names the [Four Holy Sites](places.md#the-four-holy-sites-saṁvegasthāna). The eightfold scheme (adding awakening events like the Mucalinda week, descent from Tāvatiṁsa, Parileyyaka retreat, etc.) is mostly commentarial expansion.
- **Detailed Devadatta backstory** — much of the standard psychology (jealousy from age comparison, etc.) is commentarial.

If you need to ground a claim about timing or dates that doesn't appear in the suttas linked above, you are working from commentary or modern scholarship. Be explicit about that.
